Web Performance

Objective time
vs.
Subjective time

There are really two different types of "time". The time we observe is so different from the time we measure. Key distinction. The difference between what a clock and what a human observes. The difference between a timer and watching the pot of water boil

We optimize for objective time
but that isn't very efficient.

Objective time is easy to measure, that's why we focus on it. But if the goal is to make our shit feel fast, just addressing objective time is not very efficient.

Un-Suck Passive Time

What we can do

  • Respond to users immediately
  • Give users a sense of certainty
  • Occupy users' attention
  • Render minimum viable layout
There are a lot of little things we can do to make passive waiting less shitty, I believe these 4 provide the most impact

Immediate
Response

Takes ~1 second for
flow to be disrupted.

Jakob Nielsen, Neilsen Norman Group
in a pretty famous study Jakob Nielsen of the Nielsen Norman Group showed that it took about 1 sec for peoples flow of thought to be disrupted & for them to mentally transition to a waiting state.

Let's pack as much loading into that 1 second as possible.

If it takes 1 sec for ppl to even realize they're waiting, lets pack as much as possible into that 1 second.

Mousedown event
vs.
Click event

One of my favorite ways to do that is replacing click events with mousedown events. Click events actually fire not when the mouse button goes down, but when it comes back up again. So there's this gap when the user has clicked down, but the click event has yet to fire.

Mousedown gives you a
100-150ms head start.

Stay :active, kids!

Animation duration sweet spot is 150-200ms.

Active states function just like hoverstates, but they fire when an element is clicked. They immediately give your app a sense of immediacy and responsiveness which helps to keep the user's thought flow uninterrupted. If you set the active animation duration to 150-200ms, people will keep their mouse button pressed for the full duration of the animation, so you can coax them into giving us a longer head start from our mousedown event.
